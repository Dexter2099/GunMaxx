# GunMaxx — One Pager

## Game fantasy

You clear tight combat rooms with guns and movement. Mistakes are punished quickly; learning the space and your loadout matters more than grinding stats.

## Genre

2D top-down action roguelite: fixed perspective, room-based combat, meta progression between runs, and run-based variance (rooms, pickups, modifiers).

## Design pillars

1. **Readable violence** — Enemies and hazards read clearly at a glance; deaths feel fair and fixable.
2. **Tight rooms, fast rounds** — Small spaces, short fights, frequent retries; no filler corridors.
3. **Gun-first problem solving** — Weapons and positioning solve problems; minimal mandatory systems outside combat.
4. **Controlled variance** — Runs differ enough to stay fresh without requiring huge content volume.
5. **Finish line over feature list** — Cuts beat ambition; ship a small complete loop before expanding.

## Inspirations (what is borrowed)

| Source | Borrowed |
|--------|------------|
| **Hotline Miami** | Speed, lethality, room clearing rhythm, trial-and-error mastery. |
| **Enter the Gungeon** | Top-down gun variety, dodge-and-shoot cadence, roguelite structure and meta unlocks. |

## Hard constraints

- **Offline only** — No network dependency for play, saves, or progression.
- **Single-player only** — No multiplayer, no async social features.
- **No always-online or telemetry requirement** — Privacy and portability by design.
- **Solo-first scope** — Systems and content sized for one developer and a shippable v1.

## Target experience

A disciplined loop: enter room → fight → reward → next room → die or win run → short meta hook → retry. The game should feel **sharp**, **repeatable**, and **honest about difficulty** — not padded with story, cosmetics systems, or scope that blocks shipping.
